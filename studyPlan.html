The four mandatory courses have allowed me to learn the fundamentals of the Human Computer Interaction field. Advance cognitive and social psychology for HCI for example helped me cover a wide ranges of relevant psychological topics pertaining to computer science. While I had minored in Psychology during my bachelor year, the psychological knowledge that I gained from this course not only reinforced what I have already known, but also introduced me to how certain psychological concepts are applied in the context of technology. The courses on research methods are illuminating, and I learned a lot; I had done researches before, but as a Master student, the demand for a more rigorous scientific process from what I was used to is very prevalent, which then pushed me to go above and beyond. Also, Interaction technology innovation was surprisingly fun and informative. As I have a background in computer science, I have limited knowledge on hardware, and so being able to work with controller chips, printing 3D objects, and soldering wires, I was able to apply what I know to new areas while learning new knowledge outside of my usual expertise.

As I am interested in data driven insights, half of the electives I have taken revolves around data processing and visualization. Regardless of my specific interests, knowing how to process raw data and then present it in a meaningful and creative way is a great skill to have in any fields. While I am aware that there still many novel ways of approaching data, I can say that I am much better equipped at not only understanding complex data, but also helping others do the same. Education and machine learning are the two additional themes that I have delved into during my Master journey. I learned of how existing and new technologies are currently being integrated to education to help people learn more effectively, of which the process can be made fun and interactive depending on the methods applied. Machine learning techniques, while difficult to grasp at first, gave me ways to transform and model data in ways that the traditional approaches may not be capable of.

What I really appreciate about these courses are that they are quite practical. There were still lots of theories to know about which was to be expected from a Master program, but the instructors incorporated a lot of real life examples as parts of the learning material. This really helped me with visualizing how I can apply what I learned to real-world scenarios, bridging the gap between theory and practice.
